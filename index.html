On a Friday night in the middle of June, I sat alone in my apartment that I had just recently moved into.

It was late at night when the craving hit me,

a sudden almost irresistible urge for a large bottle of Root Beer

Stay at Home

Go to 7–11

I decided to ignore my craving for a Root Beer.

I know I have a really hard time controlling myself around soda and since I was not living on my own I knew I had to be my own adult and not just buy stuff whenever I got a craving for it.

But as I sat in my apartment, I could not get the thought of an ice-cold Root Beer out of my head.

The 7–11 was only a block from my house, it would just be a short quick walk to get the Root Beer. I had only recently just finished a shift at my job and I definitely deserved a treat for myself.

I got up and grabbed my keys and my wallet and when I went to go grab my keys, I thought, I’m only going a single block and will only be gone for like 5 minutes.

I don’t need to take my phone with me everywhere.

I left my apartment with my wallet and keys in hand and went off to go and buy myself a Root Beer.

Hold Keys in Hand

Swing Keys Around

I stand in the elevator and wait as my painfully slow elevator creaks and groans it’s way up to the 3rd floor.

As I am standing in the elevator my Big Gulp in my right hand is dripping with perspiration and my hand wet from holding the cup. I reach the floor and the elevator doors open.

The Big Gulp switches hands so I can reach in my pocket to pull out my keys

I hold my keys in my hand but the perspiration from the Big Gulp makes them hard to hold on to as my keys

fall from my grasp

As I stand in the elevator, I ideally twirl my keys around my index finger.

In my absent-minded state, the keys move closer and closer to the end of my finger as the spin around it.

I obliviously sip my Root Beer as my key move ever closer to flying off my finger. The doors of the elevator open.

I move to exit the elevator and the keys that are swing around my finger fly off into the air.

I watched as in slow motion my keys slipped from my hand,

tumbled through the air,

landed with a thud on the floor right by the thin black slit tha separated theelevator and the building's floor

then I watched in horror as they slid into the dark abyss that was the elevator shaft.

Now an easy thing to do in this situation would be to just call up my roommate or parents but unfortunately, I had left my apartment with just my keys and wallet in hand and left my phone locked inside my apartment.

I was just going one block away to get a 7-11 Big Gulp. Now I was essentially stranded, all because I was missing two small items a phone and key.

So there I was, standing in the elevator trying to process my circumstances. I started running through my options.

I could call for help

but no cellphone means I can't contact anyone

I can try to get to a friend's house,

only everyone is back home for the summer

I could try to pick my lock,

but I have no idea how to pick a lock

I could try to climb in through a window,

only my apartment is three stories up and I have never climbed anything hirer than a 3-foot fence

Knock on the Neighbors' Door and ask to Borrow a Phone

Take the Train Back to my Parent's House

After a few minutes of panicking in front of my door, I decided to try and see if any of my neighbors could help me.

Knocking on the door of the apartment next to mine, a lady in her early 20s answered the door.

After explaining my situation to her, she kindly allowed me to borrow her phone to call someone.

In my panicked state, the only number I could remember was my home phone number.

After dialing the number the phone began to ringringringringringringringringringring no answer.

I gave the phone back to my neighbor and she shut the door, leaving my to my own devices.

I quickly started going into panic mode. I desperately tried to pick my lock with anything that I had on me.

By the end of my attempt, I had broken 3 random cards I had in my wallet.

I tried desperately to push or even break in my door but it would not budge

After many attempts to break into my own apartment, I sat down on the ground and just started

crying

So I sat outside my door, so close to sanctuary yet so far and tried to figure out what to do. I could not just sit in the hallway all night, I had to do something proactive.

After realizing that I could not sit inside my apartment hallway all night I decided to just go and walk around the city.

With no way of contacting anyone the only option I could come up with was to take an hour and a half train back to parents’ house in northeast Philly

hope that they were still awake by the time I got there.

Sit Outside All Night

Go To 24 Hour Diner

Finally feeling completely defeated I just sat down in front of my parent’s house.

Sitting on the doorstep to my parents’ house, in full view of anyone would look towards the house started to make me nervous so I decided that maybe it would be safer to sit out back of my house where no one could see me.

I took a seat on the cold, hard ground of my driveway and tried to get comfortable for a very long night.

As I sat outside my stomach kept rumbling.I came to the decision that it was probably better to deal with my hunger by getting some food in a diner then it was to spend the entire night sitting outside my house.

After another 30 minutes of sitting in front of my house, I decided to go to a 24-hour diner, which was at least an hour walk away.

It was 2 am when I arrived.

In the diner my company consisted of the following: in the far corner was a group of 4 people who seem to have just left a bar and were enjoying a post-drinking meal, a couple occupied booth was just quietly picking at their food and a man who I could only imagine as the owner of the tracker-trailer outside was being served by an older woman who looked to be the only person on staff that night.

I took a seat at the far edge of the counter and placed my food order and settled in for a

long night of munching french fries and sipping tea
